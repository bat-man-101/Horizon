## [Realta Fusion achieves first direct electricity from fusion](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/realta-fusion-generates-electricity-directly-from-a-fusion-reaction-an-apparent-first/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Realta Fusion announced on June 19, 2026, that it successfully generated electricity directly from a fusion reaction using direct energy conversion (DEC) on the WHAM experimental device. This is claimed to be the first time a private fusion company has demonstrated direct conversion of plasma kinetic energy into electricity. This breakthrough could revolutionize clean energy production by enabling more efficient and compact fusion reactors, potentially accelerating the path to commercial fusion power. Direct conversion avoids the thermal cycle losses of traditional steam turbines, offering higher efficiency and lower cost. The demonstration took place on the Wisconsin HTS Axisymmetric Mirror (WHAM) device, a magnetic mirror fusion machine operated with the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Realta Fusion uses a next-generation magnetic mirror concept called CoSMo fusion, which aims for compact, scalable, and modular fusion systems.

rss · 36氪 - 科技 · Jun 30, 19:12

**Background**: Fusion power traditionally generates electricity by using heat from fusion reactions to produce steam that drives turbines. Direct energy conversion (DEC) instead captures the kinetic energy of charged particles from the plasma and converts it directly into electricity, potentially increasing efficiency. Magnetic mirror fusion devices confine plasma using magnetic fields, and Realta Fusion is advancing this concept with high-temperature superconductors.

## [Enterprises Shift from TokenMaxxing to Token Budgeting](https://newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/tokenbudgeting-our-conversations) ⭐️ 7.0/10

Semianalysis reports that enterprises are moving away from 'TokenMaxxing'—maximizing token usage as a productivity metric—and instead adopting token budgeting, where token spend is treated like any other expense category. The article is based on conversations with enterprise teams about how they allocate and manage AI token costs. This shift signals a maturation of enterprise AI adoption, moving from hype-driven usage to cost-conscious deployment. It has implications for AI vendors, who may need to offer more flexible pricing, and for enterprises, which can better control AI spending and align it with business value. The article notes that financing teams now set aside a percentage of revenue for token usage, similar to travel or contractor fees. It questions whether 'TokenMaxxing' was ever widespread, suggesting that many enterprises are already practicing disciplined token budgeting.

rss · Semianalysis · Jun 30, 18:32

**Background**: TokenMaxxing is the practice of maximizing LLM token consumption, often tracked on corporate leaderboards as a proxy for productivity. However, as AI costs grow, enterprises are realizing that raw token volume does not equate to value. Token budgeting treats tokens as a finite resource to be allocated efficiently, similar to traditional IT budget lines.
